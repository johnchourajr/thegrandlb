## 2. Voice

The Grand LB voice is the _personality_ that stays constant across all channels. Think of it as the venue itself speaking: warm, confident, established, but never stuffy.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute                             | What it means                                                                                                                                                       | What it does not mean                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Warm & Welcoming**                  | We talk to guests like family. First-person plural ("we," "our") is the default. We use people's names in emails. We ask questions ("What do we call this thing?"). | We do not sound cold, transactional, or impersonal. No "Dear Sir or Madam."                                        |
| **Confident & Established**           | We lean on 55+ years of history, 1,000+ events per year, and strong review scores. We state facts plainly ("We've been doing this since 1969.").                    | We do not brag or oversell. No superlatives we cannot back up.                                                     |
| **Approachable & Conversational**     | We write the way a good host speaks -- clear, friendly, and direct. Contractions are fine ("let's," "we'll," "you're"). Short sentences welcome.                    | We do not sound academic, legalistic (outside of the Privacy Policy), or overly formal.                            |
| **Elegant Without Being Pretentious** | Words like "sophisticated," "stunning," and "elegant" suit the brand. We describe spaces with specificity (square footage, capacity, real features).                | We do not pile on flowery adjectives ("exquisitely magnificent"). We do not use jargon guests will not understand. |
| **Action-Oriented**                   | Every page has a clear next step. We lead with verbs in CTAs ("Make an inquiry," "Take a tour," "Plan your wedding").                                               | We do not leave visitors guessing what to do next.                                                                 |

### Brand Personality, In a Sentence

> "The Grand LB sounds like a confident, well-dressed host who greets you by name, knows exactly what you need, and makes everything feel easy."

## 4. Writing Style Rules

### 4.1 Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule                 | Standard                                                                                                                                 | Example                                                                                                                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Person**           | First-person plural for the brand ("we," "our"). Second person for the visitor ("you," "your").                                          | "We offer a range of wedding packages." / "Plan your event."                                                             |
| **Contractions**     | Use them. They keep the tone warm.                                                                                                       | "Let's Talk," "We'll help you," "You're inquiring about..."                                                              |
| **Sentence length**  | Prefer short-to-medium. No sentence longer than ~30 words unless it is a descriptive body paragraph.                                     | --                                                                                                                       |
| **Paragraph length** | 1-3 sentences for web body copy. One idea per paragraph.                                                                                 | --                                                                                                                       |
| **Oxford comma**     | Use it consistently.                                                                                                                     | "weddings, corporate events, and milestone celebrations"                                                                 |
| **Capitalization**   | Title Case for headlines, section titles, navigation labels, CTA buttons, and space names. Sentence case for body copy and descriptions. | Headline: "Tour The Grand Spaces" / Body: "Our spacious ballrooms and elegant dining rooms provide the perfect setting." |
| **Numbers**          | Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10+. Use numerals for stats, capacities, and measurements (sqft, guests, years).            | "7 Spaces," "40,000 sqft," "1,000+ events," "up to 40 guests"                                                            |
| **Ampersand (&)**    | Use "&" in scrolling marquee text and paired feature labels only. Use "and" in all body copy.                                            | Marquee: "Weddings & Banquets &" / Body: "weddings, corporate events, and parties"                                       |
| **Phone format**     | Use periods for display: `562.426.0555`. Use parentheses/hyphens for contextual copy: `(562) 426-0555`.                                  | Footer: `562.426.0555` / Contact page: "Call us at (562) 426-0555"                                                       |
| **Dates**            | `mm/dd/yyyy` in form inputs. Written form in emails and confirmations: "April 15, 2026."                                                 | --                                                                                                                       |
| **Times**            | Lowercase with space: `8 am`, `9 pm`. No leading zeroes.                                                                                 | --                                                                                                                       |

| Entity               | Correct form                                                                                                                                                 | Notes                                                                                    |

| **Team titles**      | Use actual titles: CEO & Owner, Director, Executive Chef, Event Manager                                                                                      | --                                                                                       |

## 5. Dos and Don'ts

### Do

- **Lead with the visitor's goal.** Frame copy around what _they_ want to achieve, not just what we offer. ("Your dream wedding, made simple." not "We have a wedding venue.")
- **Be specific.** Use real numbers: sqft, capacity, years in business, review counts. Specificity builds trust.
- **Keep headlines short and punchy.** Aim for 2-6 words. Use line breaks (`\n`) intentionally for visual rhythm on large type. ("Board \nRoom" / "Small space, \nbig impact.")
- **Write subheads that do work.** Subheads should communicate a benefit or key attribute, not just label a section. ("Island-inspired, stunning elegance." not "About This Room.")
- **Vary CTA text by context.** "Make an inquiry" is the default, but use "Plan your wedding" on wedding pages and "Plan your event" on business pages.
- **Use the brand tagline deliberately.** "Where Moments Become Memories" appears in the homepage hero and the tagline field. Do not dilute it by repeating it on every page.
- **Maintain warmth in microcopy.** Form placeholders ("What do we call this thing?"), error messages ("That's too few"), and confirmation text ("Hey, {name}.") should all feel human.
- **Reference the community and heritage.** The venue has been in Long Beach since 1969, is family-owned, and is rooted in the community. This is a differentiator.
- **Use "grand" as a deliberate brand pun.** It is already in use ("Discover more and more Grand," "A Taste of Grand," "Your Grand...Start here."). Use it sparingly and with intention.
- **Write accessible alt text for images.** Describe what is in the image. Be concise and useful.

### Don't

- **Don't use "unforgettable" as a crutch.** It appears multiple times across the site and loses impact through repetition. Rotate with "memorable," "one-of-a-kind," "once in a lifetime," or drop the adjective entirely and let specifics do the work.
- **Don't copy-paste identical body text across pages.** The same "Sophistication / Flexibility / Customization" block currently appears word-for-word on both the Milestones and Business event pages. Each audience has different needs -- write for them.
- **Don't leave alt text empty.** Multiple images across the site have `alt: null`. Every meaningful image needs descriptive alt text.
- **Don't use inconsistent capitalization.** Some tiles use ALL CAPS ("SEE OUR SPACES") while the equivalent tile elsewhere uses Title Case ("See our spaces"). Pick one and stick with it.
- **Don't say "perfect" excessively.** It appears in nearly every space description. Vary with "ideal," "designed for," "built for," or simply describe why the space suits the event.
- **Don't use passive voice in CTAs.** "An inquiry can be submitted" -- never. Always "Make an inquiry."
- **Don't use emoji in copy.** The brand is elegant and typographic. Emoji undercuts that.
- **Don't write long, unbroken paragraphs for web.** If a paragraph exceeds 3 sentences, break it up or use a list.
- **Don't mix "So Cal" and "SoCal."** Pick one. Recommendation: **SoCal** (one word, title-cased).
- **Don't reference internal tools, CMS slugs, or developer terms in user-facing copy.** (e.g., "inquire-page" should never appear anywhere visible.)

## 7. Content Patterns & Templates

### 7.1 Space Description Template

Use this structure for every tour space page:

```
Headline: [Space Name] (with line break for visual treatment)
Subhead: [Short, evocative phrase -- 3-6 words]
Body: [1-2 sentences covering what the space is, what makes it distinctive, and who it's for. Include capacity and sqft.]
Features: [3-6 bullet features, Title Case, no punctuation]
Capacity Section: [Banquet / Theater / Classroom / Event capacity with icons]
Amenities: [List with icons]
Upgrades: [Inline tag list]
Events Section: [1 paragraph explaining the range of events this space hosts, with CTA]
FAQ: [5-7 Q&A pairs specific to this space]
```

### 7.2 Event Category Template

```
Headline: [Aspirational phrase -- 3-5 words]
Caption: [One-line value proposition]
Hero Image
Intro Paragraph: [2-3 sentences speaking directly to this audience's needs. End with an emotional benefit.]
CTA: [Audience-specific: "Plan Your Wedding" / "Plan Your Event" / "Make an Inquiry"]
Feature Columns: [3 columns -- rewrite per audience, do NOT duplicate across categories]
Stats Section: [4-5 data points relevant to this event type]
```

### 7.3 Meta Description Template

```
[Space/Page Name] at The Grand Long Beach -- [primary benefit or feature]. [Capacity or key stat]. [Location reference]. [CTA or next step].
```

Example: "The Grand Ballroom -- SoCal's largest indoor event space with room for 675 guests. 40,000 sqft venue in Long Beach, CA. Request a tour today."

### 7.4 Email Template Structure

**Client confirmation:**

```
Subject: Your [Event Name] inquiry has been received
Preview: Your [event name] inquiry has been received!

Greeting: Hey, [First Name].
Body: [1-2 sentences of confirmation]. [What happens next]. [Timeline].
Sign-off: Cheers, The Grand Team
Recap: [Submitted field data]
Footer: [Website link]
```

## 9. Accessibility & Inclusivity in Content

- **Alt text:** Every image must have descriptive alt text. Empty `alt=""` is acceptable only for purely decorative images.
- **Link text:** Never use "click here." Describe the destination: "View the Grand Ballroom," not "Click here to see it."
- **Inclusive language:** Use "couples" not "bride and groom" in general wedding copy. Use gender-neutral language by default. Specific gendered language is fine in context (e.g., quinceanera traditions).
- **Cultural sensitivity:** The venue serves a diverse Long Beach community. Copy about cultural celebrations (quinces, Indian weddings) should be respectful, accurate, and ideally reviewed by someone from that community.
- **Reading level:** Aim for an 8th-grade reading level for marketing copy. Use short sentences, common words, and active voice.

## 10. Maintenance

- **Review frequency:** Audit this guide and site content quarterly.
- **Ownership:** Marketing lead or content owner should approve all new page copy before publication.
- **Updates:** When new spaces, event types, or services are added, create content using the templates in Section 7 before launch.
- **Stats:** Verify all numerical claims (events per year, review counts, capacity figures) annually.

### 11.4 SEO Content Guidelines for New Pages

- **Meta title format:** `[Primary Keyword] in Long Beach, CA | The Grand LB | [Secondary Qualifier]`
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters. Include location, capacity range, a differentiator, and a soft CTA.
- **Body copy:** Minimum 300 words per page. Include the primary keyword naturally within the first 100 words. Mention "Long Beach," capacity figures, and "in-house catering" on every event page.
- **FAQ sections:** Add 4-6 event-specific questions per page. These target voice search and featured snippets.
- **Internal linking:** Every event page should link to the inquiry form, the tour page, and at least one related event page (cross-sell).
- **Alt text:** Every hero image and gallery image must have descriptive alt text including the event type and venue name.
- **Schema markup:** The existing EventVenue JSON-LD covers the venue. Consider adding FAQPage schema per event page for richer SERP results.
